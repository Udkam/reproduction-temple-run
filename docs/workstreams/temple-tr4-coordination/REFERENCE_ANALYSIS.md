# TEMPLE-TR4 reference analysis

## Approved image

The `941x1672` north-star image shows a high-vantage portrait chase composition with a long warm causeway dominating the lower frame and dissolving into a pale distant horizon. Its transferable clean-room principles are:

- extreme near-to-far scale change and a continuous but fractured route silhouette;
- warm sandstone against a cold blue-black abyss and desaturated atmospheric distance;
- thick broken lips and downward cliff mass rather than a thin track;
- localized coral/mineral deposits and an editable white longitudinal gesture;
- a small lower-center courier, strong contact shadow, and large threat silhouettes;
- restrained HUD so route, runner, and hazards remain primary.

The image is not a runtime asset and is not copied. TR4 intentionally changes the threat lifecycle: the creature appears only during the opening and ending beats, not throughout normal play.

## Gameplay reference

The supplied Bilibili player page was inspected read-only. Its public preview confirmed a portrait runner, high rear camera, narrow elevated stone routes, severe edge danger, fast turns, and obstacle read distance. No frame or media was downloaded or stored.

The implementation uses only the general action grammar seen across public gameplay descriptions:

- jump over low roots/thresholds and broken spans;
- slide beneath low arches or transverse hazards;
- change lane around solid blockers;
- turn before route-end cliffs;
- keep each response silhouette readable before the action window.

TR4 converts those categories into original mineral-causeway geometry. It does not recreate commercial roots, statues, fires, coins, characters, monsters, UI, or map layouts.
